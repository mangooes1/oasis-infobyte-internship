MAHATMA

GANDHI

Father of the Nation and Champion of Nonviolence

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, India, is revered as the Father of the Nation in India and is celebrated worldwide for his commitment to nonviolence and justice.
Attended primary school in Porbandar and high school in Rajkot.Traveled to London, England, in 1888 to study law at University College London. Developed a strong interest in vegetarianism and joined the London Vegetarian Society, which influenced his ethical and philosophical outlook.
Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922):Urged Indians to boycott British goods, services, and institutions.
Salt March (1930): Led a 240-mile march to Dandi to produce salt, protesting British salt taxes.
Quit India Movement (1942): Demanded an end to British rule in India.
Fought against racial discrimination in South Africa and Campaigned for the abolition of untouchability in India and promoted social equality. Championed causes such as women's rights, education, and rural development and he Encouraged the use of khadi (hand-spun cloth) to promote self-reliance and reduce dependence on British goods.
Gandhi’s personal life was marked by simplicity and self-discipline. He married Kasturba Gandhi, with whom he had children, and his personal practices included a commitment to celibacy and a simple lifestyle centered around self-reliance.
Gandhi’s legacy is enduring and far-reaching. His philosophy of nonviolence has influenced numerous global movements for civil rights and freedom. He is commemorated through various memorials and honors, including the International Gandhi Peace Prize. His teachings and writings, notably “My Experiments with Truth,” continue to inspire millions around the world.
